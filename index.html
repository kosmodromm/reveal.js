Introduction to Scrum

Scrum is?

Scrum is a process framework helping team to collaborate on complex projects and products.


  • customer oriented
  • flexible
Scrum is a process framework helping team to collaborate on complex projects and products. It is flexible and has a very wide range of applications.Scrum gives the client the ability to make changes to requirements at any given time. That what makes scrum attractive to many software customers. Term "Scrum" comes from reckby, means the starting state of the teams before throwing in the ball - the minimum required set of activities, artifacts and roles on which the scrum development process is based. Scrum is usualy used on project having no algoritmic solutions. In such conditions, it is better not to think through the whole plan in advance, but to put forward hypotheses and make experiments to test them. So the team of the company's employees should be self-organized, learn from the experience, use the solution to the problem, and analyze their successes and failures to constantly improve.

What's with the team?

Scrum teams are small, usually with five to nine people.


  • product owner
  • the Scrum master
  • development team
Scrum teams are small, usually with five to nine people. The Scrum team consist of three roles: Product Owner The Scrum Master Development Team

Product owner

Defines the end goal and the tasks that it takes to get there.

The Product Owner is The person with the product vision. Product owner defines the end goal and the tasks that it takes to get there. He creates the backlog of to-do list items and reviews deliverables before the product is delivered. The Product Owner is passionate about the product and has the clearest idea of why it should exist - therefore, they will know instantly when it’s not right. They need to be clear communicators and create maximum transparency within the team. Product Owner may not “own” any individual tasks on the Scrum board but oversees all and intervenes when necessary. The Product Owner is ultimately responsible for making sure the workflow is going smoothly.

The Scrum Master

They educate teams, product owners, and the rest of the company on the intricacies of the Scrum process and try to optimize the application of this practice.

The Scrum Master - The role of the Scrum Master is not a traditional team lead or project manager he is more the role of coach.The scrum expert who helps the team build the product according to the scrum framework. They educate teams, product owners, and the rest of the company on the intricacies of the Scrum process and try to optimize the application of this practice.

Development Team

  • accept assignments
  • work collaboratively
  • consult Scrum Master
Development Team - consists of specialists who work directly on the product. Team members get stuff done. They accept assignments, work on them independently and collaboratively, consult each other and the Scrum Master when they have questions, and have to ward off distractions for the duration of the sprint. As long as the work gets done, there’s no right way to do it.

Basis of Scrum


    Sprint
  • planning
  • backlog
  • 1-4 weeks
The basis of Scrum is Sprint, during which team works on the project. Sprint is period always time limited (1-4 weeks) and has the same length throughout the life of the product. In the end of Sprint, a new working version of the product should be received. Sprint Planning is performed before the start of each Sprint , it evaluates the content of the Product Backlog and the formulate of a Sprint Backlog which contains the tasks (Story, Bugs, Tasks) that must be completed in the current sprint. Each day during a sprint, the team holds a daily scrum in which each team member answers the questions "what did I do yesterday?", "What do I plan to do today?", "What was difficult in my work?" The task of the Daily Scrum is to determine the status and progress of work on Sprint, early detection of problems and develop decisions of changing strategies to achieve Sprint's goals.

Sprint Review & Sprint Retrospective

  • assessment of team productivity
  • efficiency
  • problems
At the end of Sprint, Sprint Review and Sprint Retrospective are produced, the purpose of which is to estimate the productivity of the team in the last Sprint, predict the expected efficiency (performance) in the next sprint, detect existing problems Each sprint must have a goal that motivates and is achieved by completing tasks from the Sprint Backlog.

Advantages

  • responsive
  • flexible
  • easy to learn
  • each sprint = working product
Scrum is customer centric, responsive. Scrum gives the client the ability to make changes to requirements at any given time. Scrum is quite easy to learn and saves time by eliminating non-critical activities. Scrum allows you to get a potentially working product at the end of each Sprint.

Limitations

  • flexible is not so good...
  • self-organizing, multi-functional team is rare
Scrum belongs to the Agile family, so Scrum welcomes changes in requirements at any time (Product backlog can be changed at any time). This makes it difficult to use Scrum in fixed-cost / fixed-time projects.The Scrum ideology argues that it is impossible to predict all upcoming changes, so there is no point in planning project in advance, limiting planning only the work that needs to be done in the current Sprint. Another weakness in Scrum is its emphasis on a self-organizing, multi-functional team. According to research by sociologists, the number of self-motivated employees able to be self-organized is less then 15% of the working population.
